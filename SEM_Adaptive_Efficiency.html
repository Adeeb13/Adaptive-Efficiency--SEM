The Equations That
Define Self-Improvement

Every claim in this framework is formally grounded. No metaphor without mathematics. Click any equation to expand its physical derivation and meaning.

EQ. 01  ·  CORE DEFINITION
ADAPTIVE EFFICIENCY
AE(t) = dC(t) / dt
The instantaneous rate of change of the Capability Function. Unlike thermodynamic efficiency, AE has no Carnot ceiling — it is bounded only by resource, noise, and information-theoretic limits.
C(t) is a weighted composite: C(t) = α·Ccog(t) + β·Cphys(t), where α = 0.7, β = 0.3. The benchmark suite B(t) is dynamic — harder tasks are introduced as capability grows, preventing C(t) from saturating on a fixed distribution. This is what distinguishes AE from all static performance metrics.
OPEN SYSTEM
EQ. 02  ·  CAPABILITY FUNCTION
WEIGHTED COMPOSITE
C(t) = α·Ccog + β·Cphys
Cognitive and physical capability components, weighted α=0.7 and β=0.3, measured against a dynamic benchmark B(t) that scales with the agent's improvement.
Ccog measures performance on reasoning, coding, and scientific problem-solving. Cphys covers embodied morphological tasks. The α/β = 7/3 ratio means the AE phase portrait gradient is steeper in the cognitive direction — prioritize cognitive optimization under resource constraints.
COMPOSITE METRIC
EQ. 03  ·  STOCHASTIC DYNAMICS
PHYSICAL REALITY MODEL
C(t+1) = C(t) + F(t)·[λeff·C·(1−C/Cmax) − δK] + σ·C·ξ(t)
The full stochastic model incorporating Gaussian noise ξ(t)∼N(0,1), failure events F(t)∼Bernoulli(1−pfail), logistic saturation, and the algorithmic incompressibility correction δK(t).
The logistic saturation term C·(1−C/Cmax) is deliberately analogous to resource-limited population dynamics and thermodynamic phase transitions. λeff = α·λcog + β·λphys. The δK(t) term increases monotonically with agent complexity, encoding the Kolmogorov incompressibility ceiling.
STOCHASTIC
EQ. 04  ·  LANDAUER BOUND
THERMODYNAMIC FLOOR
Eerase ≥ kB · T · ln(2)
≈ 2.87 × 10⁻²¹ joules at T = 300K. Every self-modification cycle that erases a candidate has a non-zero thermodynamic cost. The DGM archive strategy is thermodynamically optimal.
Three engineering consequences: (1) retained modifications are thermodynamically cheaper than erased ones; (2) indefinite self-improvement at zero cost is physically impossible; (3) more compressible agent descriptions require less energy to modify — compressed, general representations are thermodynamically favoured over verbose, specialised ones.
LANDAUER 1961
EQ. 05  ·  CARNOT COMPARISON
THERMODYNAMIC CEILING
ηCarnot = 1 − Tcold / Thot
AE operates in a categorically different domain from thermodynamic efficiency. Carnot bounds closed systems. AE bounds open systems that import free energy and export entropy — exactly as living systems do.
The SEM is a Prigogine dissipative structure: it imports computational energy and information, exports heat and discarded modifications, and — if operating above the sufficient condition threshold — increases its internal organizational complexity. This is not metaphor; it is the same mathematical structure as Bénard convection cells and biological metabolism.
PRIGOGINE ANALOG
EQ. 06  ·  SUFFICIENT CONDITION
THEOREM CORE
λeff > σ²/2 + pfail·μfail + δK,max / C0
The sufficient condition for sustained positive AE over an interval [0, T]. When this inequality holds, the system is guaranteed to self-improve on average, regardless of noise realisation.
Three terms on the right: σ²/2 is the noise dominance threshold (FR-1), pfail·μfail is the expected capability loss per failure cycle, and δK,max/C₀ is the normalised algorithmic incompressibility correction. All three are empirically estimable. The theorem is falsifiable.

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

Six Layers.
One Closed Loop.

The SEM is not a model — it is a closed-loop architecture where safety is embedded thermodynamically inside the evolution cycle, not appended as an external guardrail.

LAYER 01
Environmental State Encoder
Physics-aware embedding of task signals, sensor data, and performance feedback into structured vectors for the DGM core.
LAYER 02
DGM Cognitive Core
Foundation-model-driven self-modification engine. Generates candidate rewrites of the agent's own codebase. Operates in open-ended archive mode for second-order improvement.
LAYER 03
AE Evaluator
Computes AE(t) = dC(t)/dt by benchmarking the candidate agent. Accept if AE_new > 0; archive and revert otherwise.
LAYER 04
Agent Archive
All variants — successful and suboptimal — are stored with metadata. Thermodynamically optimal per Landauer: retaining is cheaper than erasing.
LAYER 05
Physical Substrate (MSRR)
For embodied implementations: Modular Self-Reconfiguring Robotics interface. Issues morphological directives on cognitive command.
LAYER 06
Safety Monitor
Alignment as thermodynamic constraint, not external override. Enforces resource bounds (C_max), objective immutability, and the sufficient condition for positive AE.

The State Encoder transforms raw environmental signals into structured embeddings. For physical tasks, the encoding is physics-aware — geometric constraints, energy budgets, and material properties are represented explicitly. For cognitive tasks, the encoder adds domain tags, difficulty estimates, and historical performance vectors that inform the DGM core's modification strategy.

FORMAL RESULT

The Adaptive Efficiency
Growth Theorem

"Let a Self-Evolving Machine operate under the stochastic model. Necessary and sufficient conditions for sustained expected positive AE, together with three explicit failure regimes, follow from the dynamics of the Capability Function under Gaussian noise, failure probability, resource saturation, and algorithmic incompressibility."

NECESSARY CONDITION FOR E[AE(t)] > 0
λeff · C · (1 − C/Cmax) > δK(t) + pfail · μfail · C
SUFFICIENT CONDITION FOR SUSTAINED AE > 0 ON [0, T]
λeff > σ²/2 + pfail·μfail + δK,max/C0
FR1
NOISE DOMINANCE
σ²/2 > λeff − pfail·μfail
Analog of the order-disorder phase transition in spin glasses. Below the critical signal-to-noise ratio, no improving state is stable. Stochastic fluctuations overwhelm the systematic improvement signal.
FR2
RESOURCE SATURATION
C(t) → Cmax ⇒ AE → −δK < 0
The logistic growth ceiling. As capability approaches Cmax, the improvement gradient shrinks and the incompressibility correction dominates. More compute delays but does not eliminate this regime.
FR3
ALGORITHMIC LIMIT
δK(t) ≥ λeff·C·(1−C/Cmax)
The deepest failure mode, grounded in Chaitin-Kolmogorov theory. Escape requires not more iterations of the same search, but a second-order innovation in the modification strategy itself.
